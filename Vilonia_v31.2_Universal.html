About Vilonia
The story behind the name, the app, and the people who built her
🌪️
The namesake
On April 27, 2014, an EF4 tornado tore through the communities of Vilonia and Mayflower, Arkansas — two small towns north of Little Rock. The storm produced peak winds of approximately 180 mph and claimed 16 lives.

The Vilonia-Mayflower tornado was part of a broader outbreak across the mid-South that day. It was a reminder that violent tornadoes are not confined to the Great Plains — they can and do strike anywhere, anytime.

This app carries that name as a tribute to those communities, and as a commitment to taking severe weather seriously.
⚡
About the app
Vilonia is an AI-powered weather hub built for everyone — from casual weather watchers to hardcore storm nerds. She brings together live NEXRAD radar, real NWS alerts, forecasts, air quality, allergy data, activity planning, a comprehensive weather events database, and an AI meteorologist you can actually talk to.

Vilonia is currently in private beta testing. She is not yet a public product. Every feature you see is being actively tested and refined.

Built with care. Powered by real data. Named after a real storm.
👤
About the creator

SimplyMadness is a content creator on YouTube, an artist, and someone who genuinely believes that understanding the atmosphere can save lives. She has 3 siblings, a cat, and an encyclopedic knowledge of significant tornado events that would make most meteorologists jealous.
